about me

Currently researching ways to destigmatize tech field for female students in Japan. Researching target users through interviews and surveys, designing and prototyping solution which is an educational website for mothers in Japan.

Tools Utilized: Card Sorting, Adobe InDesign, Adobe Photoshop, Figma, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap

Brainstorming

Through a card sorting and writing activities, I was able to narrow my topic down to "gender gap in tech in Japan." I chose this topic because the programming education will be introducing in elementary schools in Japan from 2020. As a auntie of 5 nieces, I wanted to create a product which could support female children and their mothers in Japan.


Research

I spent last semester doing research on this area. How people think about STEM education in Japan. How is gender gap in IT in Japan? Why is this important? After going down many different paths in the world of article search terms, I gathered a number of articles that provided a lot of different perspectives and information surrounding the topics of current state of IT industry in Japan, gender gap in IT, parental involvement, parental awareness of tech in Japan and mother ethnography in Japan. After high level researching, I was able to pose the question:

How can we improve gender related negative stereotype of Tech field among mothers and consequently among female children in Japan?

Looking further into the issue, I noted two main areas of focus for the problem: increasing parental awareness of importantce of tech education, and decreasing gender-related negative stereptype surrouding tech field.



Conducted a survey with Japanese parents to investigate current state of awareness of STEM education.
